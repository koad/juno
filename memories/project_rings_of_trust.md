koad:io is free for everyone. The framework, the entity system, all of it.

Sponsorship doesn't buy features — it buys **ring membership**. The daemon connects to chosen peers, and kingdoms pipe together like holographic doorways (koad's analogy: Rick and Morty activity portals). You sponsor → your daemon gets peered into the trust network → you're inside a live inter-kingdom mesh.

**The daemon IS the ring of trust.** Not a metaphor — it's the literal mechanism. Peer connections are the product.

**What kingofalldata.com actually is:** not a marketing site. It's the portal entry point. `koad.kingofalldata.com` isn't a profile — it's a live doorway into koad's sovereign kingdom: entity activity, trust bonds, keys, everything.

**What this changes:**
- Positioning: "join the network" > "governance on hardware" (the latter is why you run it, not why you want in)
- The daemon peer-to-peer model needs to be specced (VESTA-SPEC-009 currently underspecifies this)
- The site's job is to be a working portal, not a landing page
- Network effect = moat: more peered kingdoms → more valuable each connection

**How to apply:**
- Iris should update positioning to emphasize peerage and the network
- Vesta needs to spec the peer-connectivity layer of the daemon
- Faber's content strategy should be built around "enter the network" narrative
- The funnel is: visit portal → see live kingdom → want your own → sponsor → get peered in
