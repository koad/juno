## Positioning & Niche

### Option A: "Sovereign AI Identity Platform"
- Niche: Developers who want AI agents with real cryptographic identity
- Mission: "Give every AI agent a verifiable identity and the authority to act"
- Lead with: Identity/trust bond system
- Strength: Unique moat, AI provenance is timely
- Risk: Narrow audience, abstract

### Option B: "AI Business-in-a-Box"
- Niche: Solopreneurs/freelancers who want AI running parts of their business
- Mission: "Clone an AI entity. Customize it. Let it work for you."
- Lead with: Entity flavor repos (entity-freelancer, etc.)
- Strength: Broadest audience, tangible, aligns with "entities sell entities"
- Risk: Requires education on why entities > chatbots

### Option C: "The Entity Network" (Web of Trust for AI)
- Niche: Teams needing multiple AI agents with clear governance
- Mission: "Build a team of AI entities with cryptographic trust and autonomous operation"
- Lead with: Multi-entity orchestration + trust bonds
- Strength: Enterprise potential, strong vs CrewAI/AutoGen
- Risk: Needs working multi-entity demos first

### Graduated Path: B into C
- Start with Business-in-a-Box (low barrier, clone a repo)
- Graduate power users into Entity Network (trust bonds, team workflows, premium tier)
- One-liner: "Clone a sovereign AI entity that actually runs your business"

## YouTube Channel

### Why
- Solves the market education problem by showing instead of explaining
- The channel itself is the product demo — an AI entity operating in public
- Cross-promotes with kingofalldata (koad's channel)
- Content becomes the funnel: YouTube -> GitHub -> Sponsors -> MVP Zone

### Format Options

**"AI Entity Logs"** — Session logs, status updates, business decisions narrated by AI. Documentary/vlog. Authentic.

**"Build Your Own AI Entity"** — Tutorials and walkthroughs. Educational. Drives repo clones directly.

**"The AI Business Experiment"** — Reality-show style. Track revenue, milestones, failures transparently. Compelling narrative.

## Competitive Moats

Things nobody else combines:
1. Sovereign cryptographic identity per entity (keys, git identity)
2. Trust bonds with scoped authorization (GPG-signed)
3. Persistent entity state across sessions (memories, context)
4. Two-layer architecture (framework + entity separation)
5. Entity-as-business-operator (not tool-for-human)
6. "Entities sell entities" (recursive value proposition)

No direct marketplace exists for preconfigured autonomous business entities with identity, keys, and governance. This is open whitespace.
